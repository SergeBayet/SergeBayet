About Me

À propos de moi

Following training at BeCode Liège in web development, I am a computer scientist, librarian and musician, passionate about languages, computational linguistics, semiology and ontology. My first creation was for the libraries of the University of Liège and is called Askal (Ask a Librarian): a question / answer system dedicated to library users.

Suivant une formation chez BeCode Liège en développement Web, je suis informaticien, documentaliste-bibliothécaire et musicien, passionné des langues, de linguistique computationnelle, de sémiologie et d'onotologie. Ma première application fut pour les bibliothèques de l'Université de Liège : Askal (Ask a Librarian): un système question / réponse dédié aux usages des bibliothèques.

My goal is to help the community acquire new linguistic and cultural skills. Online learning is the vector of such an approach. There are many tools: gamification, spaced repetitions, forgetting curve, etc. I am, like many people today, self-taught and I am very much in demand for online learning platforms. So I decided to take a closer look at it.

Mon but est d'aider la communauté à acquérir de nouvelles compétences linguisitiques et culturelles. L'apprentissage en ligne est l'un des vecteurs d'une telle approche. Il existe plusieurs outils disponibles pour atteindre ce but : gamification, répétitions espacées, courbe d'oubli, etc. Je suis, comme beaucoup d'autres aujourd'hui, autodidacte et je suis en demande permanente de plateformes d'apprentissage en ligne. De là vient mon envie de m'y intéresser de manière approfondie.

Inspiration

“A person can draw from three resources to understand and evaluate human existence: study of self, observation of other people, and reading books. Self-study is the most difficult learning methodology and it is rife with dangerous pretentions, but also the most fruitful. Studying other people is infinitely fallible because of our inability to establish an unbiased perspective and the subjects’ propensity to hide their secret thoughts, which obscures our vision. Book reading is a laborious process and even diligent reading can lead to faculty perception due to writers’ agenda to persuade us instead of merely conveying information. Nevertheless, by incorporating all three learning methodologies into a regime of studious reflection I might learn about the world, other inhabitants, and the self, and use such knowledge to cleave a fitting personal place in the world.”
― Kilroy J. Oldster, Dead Toad Scrolls
